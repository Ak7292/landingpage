Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a popular dish from Bihar, India. Litti is a wheat flour cake that has been salted and baked, filled with sattu (fried gram flour) and some special spices, and served with ghee. It is similar to Bati in appearance but there is a little difference. Litti is best eaten with brinjal bharta or mashed potato. Litti-Chokha is now making its presence internationally.

Indulge in the authentic flavors of Bihar with our signature dish, Litti Chokha. A culinary delight cherished across the heartlands of India, this traditional Bihari delicacy is a symphony of rustic spices and wholesome ingredients. Picture soft, roasted wheat balls stuffed with a tantalizing mix of sattu (roasted gram flour), aromatic spices, and a hint of tanginess from mustard oil. Accompanied by the quintessential chokha a fiery mash of roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and potatoes, infused with garlic, green chilies, and coriander leaves, it's a culinary adventure that transports you to the bustling streets and vibrant kitchens of Bihar. Savor the rich heritage and cultural essence of Bihar with every bite of our Litti Chokha, a gastronomic journey that celebrates tradition and taste.
